Employee Handbook - Company Culture and Policies

AUTHENTICHECK EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK
Edition 4.2 - Effective February 2026

Welcome to AuthentiCheck!

We are committed to building a diverse, inclusive, and innovative workplace where our talented team can thrive. This handbook provides guidelines for employment and workplace conduct.

CORE VALUES:
1. Integrity - We prioritize honesty and transparency
2. Excellence - We strive for the highest standards
3. Innovation - We embrace new ideas and technologies
4. Collaboration - We work together to achieve goals
5. Responsibility - We are accountable for our actions

EMPLOYMENT POLICIES:

Equal Employment Opportunity:
AuthentiCheck is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

At-Will Employment:
Employment is at-will and can be terminated with or without cause by either party.

Work Schedule:
- Standard: 40 hours per week
- Flexible hours available with manager approval
- Remote work: 3 days per week permitted
- Core hours: 10 AM - 3 PM

Compensation & Benefits:
- Competitive salaries based on market rates
- Annual performance bonuses (up to 20%)
- Health insurance (medical, dental, vision)
- 401(k) plan with company match
- Paid Time Off: 20 days annually
- Parental leave: 12 weeks paid

Professional Development:
- $1,500 annual training budget
- Tuition reimbursement program
- Internal mentoring programs
- Conference attendance support
- LinkedIn Learning access

Code of Conduct:
- Professional behavior at all times
- No harassment, discrimination, or bullying
- Confidentiality of company information
- Conflict of interest disclosure
- Social media responsibility

Health & Safety:
- Safe work environment commitment
- Ergonomic workstation setup
- Mental health support services
- Wellness programs and gym membership
- Emergency procedures and training

Data Security & Privacy:
- Password protection requirements
- Confidentiality agreements
- Data breach reporting procedures
- BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) policy
- Acceptable use of company resources

Grievance Procedures:
- Open-door policy with managers
- HR investigation of formal complaints
- Whistleblower protection
- Non-retaliation policy

Disciplinary Action:
Progressive discipline approach:
1. Verbal warning
2. Written warning
3. Final warning
4. Termination

Company Communications:
- All-hands meetings: Monthly
- Department meetings: Weekly
- Company intranet: SharePoint
- Slack: Primary communication tool
